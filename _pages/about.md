# About Me

I am an Aeronautical Engineer with an M.Sc. in Engineering Sciences (Aerospace Engineering) from the National University of Córdoba, currently in the final year of my PhD. My research focuses on the development and calibration of an optimized computational methodology for blast load simulation.

### CFD & PhD
I have developed a solver capable of accurately predicting explosion-induced loads on structures without the need for complex chemistry modeling. This high-fidelity yet computationally efficient approach drastically reduces costs, enabling the analysis of explosion effects on large-scale structures and vast domains where traditional chemical-reactive simulations would be prohibitive.

My workflow is centered on OpenFOAM, with specific expertise in the rhoCentralFoam solver. Working in CFD for explosions requires a multidisciplinary skill set that I apply to every project: from advanced fluid mechanics (gas dynamics) and numerical methods to the physics of explosions—including shock waves, blast waves, and wave reflection.

### Teaching as a Driver for Innovation
Unlike many purely technical engineers, I consider my background in university teaching (Fluid Mechanics and Structures) to be a key competitive advantage. Being an Assistant Professor is more than sharing knowledge; it is a rigorous process that requires me to:

* **Master the Fundamentals**: To explain complex concepts simply, one must first understand them perfectly. In the field of CFD, it is easy to become preoccupied with numerical outputs and lose sight of the underlying physics. Teaching Fluid Mechanics constantly brings me back to the foundational principles, ensuring that every simulation is backed by a rigorous physical understanding. 

* **Effective Technical Communication**: The ability to convey complex simulation results to diverse audiences—whether academic peers, industrial clients, or multidisciplinary teams—is a skill I refine daily by presenting to classrooms of over 100 students.

* **Constant Update**: Engaging with new generations of students and integrating emerging tools like AI into technical education keeps me at the methodological forefront. I firmly believe that the fresh perspectives of students, combined with the collaborative exchange with fellow professors, are essential drivers of innovation and growth.
I am highly motivated, enjoy teamwork, and thrive in projects that push the boundaries of current numerical simulation.

### Independent Consulting & Industry Experience
Throughout my career, I have led and collaborated on independent structural calculation projects for the Aerospace and Nuclear industries in Argentina, working alongside specialized colleagues from the National University of Córdoba. Utilizing Ansys Mechanical, I provide high-level technical support for critical components where safety and structural integrity are paramount.

My core responsibilities in these high-stakes sectors include: geometry preparation, FEM Simulation, Design Optimization and Technical Reporting.
