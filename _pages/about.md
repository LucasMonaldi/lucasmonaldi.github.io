# About Me

I am an Aeronautical Engineer and currently a PhD Candidate focused on the **structural vulnerability of critical infrastructure against external explosions**. My work combines the power of **CFD (OpenFOAM)** with complex structural analysis to transform extreme physical phenomena into actionable engineering data.

### CFD & Explosion Consulting
Throughout my career, I have specialized in using cutting-edge tools such as **OpenFOAM, Ansys Fluent, and Ansys Mechanical** to solve multidisciplinary challenges in aerodynamics, gas dynamics, and fluid mechanics. My goal is to provide innovative solutions to complex real-world problems, integrating shock wave modeling and dynamic structural response.

### My Differential: Teaching as a Driver for Innovation
Unlike many purely technical engineers, I consider my background in university teaching (**Fluid Mechanics and Structures**) to be a key competitive advantage. 

Being an Assistant Professor does not just mean sharing knowledge; it forces me to:
* **Master the Fundamentals:** To explain complex concepts simply, I must first understand them perfectly.
* **Effective Technical Communication:** The ability to explain CFD simulation results to clients or multidisciplinary teams is a skill I have honed in front of hundreds of students.
* **Constant Update:** Interacting with new generations and integrating AI into technical education keeps me at the methodological forefront.

I am highly motivated, enjoy teamwork, and thrive in projects that push the boundaries of current numerical simulation.
