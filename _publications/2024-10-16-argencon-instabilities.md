*English title: Development of instabilities in explosions in closed enclosures*

### Project Overview
This research investigates the shock wave generated by an instantaneous energy release at the center of a closed domain, focusing on its subsequent rebounds and reflections. The study specifically analyzes the **Richtmyer-Meshkov instability (RMI)**, which occurs when shock waves interact with a central low-density zone, leading to the breakdown of flow symmetry.

**Key Highlights:**
* **Numerical Simulation:** Performed using the **rhoCentralFoam** solver in **OpenFOAM**, solving the Euler equations with the Kurganov-Noelle-Petrova (KNP) scheme.
* **Symmetry Breaking:** The study characterizes how and when the initial spherical symmetry of the explosion is lost due to fluid instabilities during multiple reflections.
* **Mach Number Influence:** Simulations were conducted for shock Mach numbers of $M_s = 2$, $5$, and $10$, comparing the onset and development of the instability across different energy levels.
* **Validation:** Numerical results, including the symmetry-breaking point and reflected pressures on the walls, were validated against previously published literature.
