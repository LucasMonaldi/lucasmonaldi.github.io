*English title: Considerations on enthalpy variation in steady and unsteady gas dynamics*

### Project Overview
This research explores the evolution of stagnation enthalpy ($h_0$) in isentropic gas flows. While it is a well-known principle that $h_0$ remains constant in steady flows, this work derives and verifies expressions for its variation in unsteady (time-dependent) regimes.

**Key Highlights:**
* **Theoretical Derivation:** Development of mathematical expressions to describe how stagnation enthalpy fluctuates when the flow field changes over time.
* **Numerical Verification:** Use of **OpenFOAM** to solve the Euler equations and validate the theoretical findings through high-fidelity simulations.
* **Key Result:** The numerical results accurately confirm that in unsteady flows, the local change in pressure over time ($\partial p / \partial t$) is the primary driver of stagnation enthalpy variation.
* **Solver Accuracy:** Demonstrated the capability of the KNP scheme within OpenFOAM to track thermodynamic properties in complex, time-dependent scenarios.
