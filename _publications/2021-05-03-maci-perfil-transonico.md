*English title: Numerical simulation of the flow around an aerodynamic profile in transonic regime*

### Project Overview
This study focuses on the numerical simulation of the **RAE 2822 supercritical airfoil** in transonic flow conditions using **ANSYS Fluent**. The research evaluates the performance of different turbulence models in capturing complex phenomena such as shock-boundary layer interactions.

**Key Highlights:**
* **Turbulence Modeling:** Compared several models, including Spalart-Allmaras, $k-\omega$ SST, and Transition SST. The **Spalart-Allmaras** model showed the best overall performance for this specific transonic case.
* **Validation:** Results for lift ($C_L$) and drag ($C_D$) coefficients, pressure distribution ($C_p$), and boundary layer velocity profiles were validated against experimental wind tunnel data from AGARD reports.
* **Shock Wave Capture:** The simulation accurately predicted the shock wave position at approximately 53% of the chord ($x/c \approx 0.53$).
* **Numerical Strategy:** Implemented a mixed meshing strategy and a small trailing edge chamfer to ensure high mesh quality ($y^+ < 1$) within the constraints of student-version software.
