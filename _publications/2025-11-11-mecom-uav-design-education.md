*English title: Experiences in the use of numerical methods and simulation for the design of an unmanned aerial vehicle in educational instances*

### Project Overview
This paper describes the application of systems engineering methodologies to the design of an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) for delivering small and fragile cargo within a university setting. The research focuses on integrating numerical methods and simulations throughout the conceptual, preliminary, and detailed design phases.

**Key Highlights:**
* **Educational Innovation:** Implementation of Project-Based Learning (PBL) in advanced engineering courses, focusing on the real-world challenges of aeronautical design.
* **Systems Engineering:** Application of AS9100 and NADCAP standards to the iterative design process, covering components like wings, fuselage, and systems integration.
* **Numerical Tools:** Use of simulation and numerical analysis as primary decision-making tools to resolve engineering trade-offs and technical uncertainties.
* **Methodology:** Detailed breakdown of the design cycle, from initial mission requirements and cargo constraints to final engineering solutions.
