*English title: Unsteady cylindrical shock wave reflection over a straight wall using OpenFOAM*

### Project Overview
This research investigates the complex interaction of a variable-velocity shock wave hitting a flat surface. Unlike planar shocks, a cylindrical shock wave generated by a sudden energy release creates an unsteady reflection process where the incident angle and Mach number change continuously.

**Key Highlights:**
* **Numerical Setup:** The simulations were performed using the **rhoCentralFoam** solver in **OpenFOAM**, implementing the Kurganov-Noelle-Petrova (KNP) central-upwind scheme.
* **Physics of Unsteadiness:** The study focuses on a blast source located $0.8$ m from the wall, tracking the transition and evolution of the reflection patterns.
* **Validation:** Numerical results (reflection angle and Mach number) were compared against pseudo-steady theoretical models, showing excellent agreement in capturing the wave's curvature and strength.
* **CFD Capabilities:** Demonstrated the robustness of the KNP scheme in handling high-pressure gradients and complex shock-to-shock interactions.


**Note:** This is a **peer-reviewed full paper** published in the congress proceedings.
