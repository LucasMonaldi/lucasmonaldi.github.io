*English title: Numerical study on unsteady shock wave reflection in three-dimensional configurations*

### Project Overview
While many shock reflection studies are limited to 2D simplified models, real-world explosions are inherently three-dimensional. This paper presents a numerical investigation of a spherical shock wave, traveling at variable velocity, reflecting off a solid surface in a 3D domain.

**Key Highlights:**
* **3D Numerical Simulation:** Performed using the **rhoCentralFoam** solver within the **OpenFOAM** library, solving the Euler equations for compressible flow.
* **Algorithm:** Implementation of the second-order Kurganov, Noelle, and Petrova (KNP) central-upwind scheme to ensure high-resolution capture of the shock front.
* **Validation:** The 3D numerical results were validated against experimental data and pseudo-analytical models, showing that the simulation accurately captures the transition stages of the reflection process.
* **Physics of Spherical Shocks:** Analysis of how the spherical expansion affects the overpressure and the triple point trajectory compared to 2D approximations.



**Note:** This is a **peer-reviewed full paper** published in the congress proceedings.
