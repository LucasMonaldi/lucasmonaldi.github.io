*English title: Validation of the rhoCentralFoam solver for predicting loads on fuel storage tanks from explosions*

### Project Overview
This study validates the use of the **Kurganov-Noelle-Petrova (KNP)** numerical scheme within the **rhoCentralFoam** solver (OpenFOAM) for simulating blast waves and their interaction with industrial storage tanks. The model employs a simplified "instantaneous energy release" approach to predict the pressure loads that could lead to structural failure.

**Key Highlights:**
* **Industrial Application:** The research is focused on the safety of fuel storage facilities, specifically analyzing how blast overpressure ($P_{so}$) impacts cylindrical tank geometries.
* **Solver Validation:** Verification of the solver's ability to capture complex wave phenomena such as Mach stem formation and pressure reflections on curved surfaces.
* **Methodology:** Implementation of high-resolution CFD models to replace or complement traditional empirical methods (like the TNT equivalent method) in risk assessment.
* **Safety Engineering:** The results provide a validated computational framework for determining safety distances and designing protective measures for critical energy infrastructure.
