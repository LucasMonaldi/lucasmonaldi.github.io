*English title: Numerical analysis of shock wave reflection on straight walls*

### Project Overview
This study evaluates the predictive capability of the **rhoCentralFoam** solver within **OpenFOAM** for calculating reflected pressures generated by the impact of shock waves on solid walls. The research is specifically designed to validate numerical methodologies for the design of blast protection structures and risk assessment in high-risk industries like Oil & Gas.

**Key Highlights:**
* **Experimental Validation:** Numerical results were compared against experimental data from hemispherical explosive charges and overpressure sensors (Ridoux et al.), showing high correlation.
* **High-Performance Computing (HPC):** The simulations utilized high-resolution meshes of approximately **13 million elements** to accurately capture the shock front and pressure peaks.
* **Numerical Configuration:** Implemented the Kurganov, Noelle, and Petrova (KNP) scheme with a $CFL$ number of $0.5$ to ensure stability and precision in the high-gradient regions.
* **Practical Applications:** The methodology provides a robust tool for calculating the structural load on protective barriers and evaluating safety distances in industrial explosion scenarios.
